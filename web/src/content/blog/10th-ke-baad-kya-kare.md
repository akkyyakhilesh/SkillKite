## 10th ke baad sabse pehla sawaal

"Science lu ya Commerce?" — yeh sawaal har student ke dimag mein hota hai. Aur zyada tar log yeh decision apne relatives ki advice pe lete hain. Problem yeh hai ki unhe bhi pura picture nahi pata hota.

Is post mein hum **saare options** cover karenge — bina kisi bias ke. Na "Science best hai" bolenge, na "Commerce easy hai." Honest comparison, taki tum apna decision khud le sako.

## Option 1 — Science (PCM / PCB)

**PCM (Physics, Chemistry, Maths)** opens the door to engineering, NDA, merchant navy, architecture, and pure science degrees. If you enjoy problem-solving and maths doesn't scare you, this is a strong path.

**PCB (Physics, Chemistry, Biology)** is for medicine (NEET), paramedical courses, biotechnology, and agriculture science. If you're drawn to healthcare or life sciences, this is your lane.

**Honest take:** Science is demanding. If you're choosing it only because "log kehte hain Science lena chahiye," reconsider. A student who loves Commerce will outperform a reluctant Science student every time.

## Option 2 — Commerce

Commerce covers accounting, business studies, economics, and finance. It leads to CA, CS, CMA, BBA, B.Com, and banking careers.

**Who should pick this:** If you're comfortable with numbers but prefer business logic over physics equations, Commerce is a great fit. It's also the path to entrepreneurship — understanding money is understanding business.

**Myth-busting:** "Commerce mein scope nahi hai" is completely wrong. A qualified CA earns more than most engineers. The key is picking a professional qualification (CA/CS/CMA) early and committing to it.

## Option 3 — Arts / Humanities

Arts covers history, political science, psychology, sociology, languages, and fine arts. It leads to law (CLAT), UPSC, journalism, design, social work, and teaching.

**Honest take:** Arts gets the least respect in Indian families, but some of the highest-paying and most impactful careers come from this stream — IAS officers, lawyers, psychologists, UX designers. The catch: you need to pair Arts with a clear professional goal.

## Option 4 — Polytechnic / ITI / Diploma

Not interested in 11th-12th? You can enter a 3-year polytechnic diploma right after 10th and start working as a junior engineer or technician. ITI courses are shorter (1-2 years) and lead to skilled trades.

**Who should consider this:** If you want to start earning sooner, prefer hands-on work, or your family needs financial support quickly. A polytechnic diploma holder can later do lateral entry into B.Tech (join directly in 2nd year).

## How to actually decide

Don't pick a stream based on:
- What your cousin did
- What "has scope" (every stream has scope if you're good at it)
- What's "easy" (nothing worth doing is easy)

Instead, ask yourself:
1. **Which subjects do I genuinely enjoy studying?** Not "tolerate" — enjoy.
2. **What kind of work excites me?** Building things? Helping people? Solving business problems? Creating art?
3. **What are my real constraints?** Family finances, location, available colleges.
