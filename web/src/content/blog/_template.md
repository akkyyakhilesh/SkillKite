## First section heading

Open with the question the reader actually typed into Google. Keep paragraphs
short (2-4 lines). Honest tone — same "don't oversell" voice as the bot's guides.

Use **bold** for key terms and the occasional myth-bust. Aim for 800-1200 words,
scannable, with H2 sections every few paragraphs.

## A second section

Break options or ideas into their own headings so the page is skimmable.

- Bullet lists work for quick comparisons
- Each point one line where possible

### A sub-point (H3)

H3 renders in saffron — use it for named options inside a section (e.g. a single
career, course, or stream).

> Use a blockquote for a real student question or a punchy honest take. It gets a
> saffron left-border and stands out from the body.

## How to actually decide

Close the body with a practical "how to think about this" section — not a hard
recommendation. Give the reader a framework, not an order.
